The Background

I find presentations skills an extremely interesting subject, if you think about it, in a way that's how early documentaries started, as a travel logue slide shows, telling stories with a first person voice over account of the trip using still images to illustrate. Very similar to a modern digital story.

And those presentation of the time is said to have inspired films like Nanook of the North.

And  even early motion pictures cameras like the Lumiere once, you could film, develop the footage and reversing the lens it could be turned into a projector to show the rushes.



In 1895, the Lumière brothers took their camera to a meeting of the Congress of Photographic Societies.
They filmed the members’ arrival at the conference, and then developed the footage and showed it to them that afternoon.
and I can't think of any better way to quiet litterally "draw the audience in" and capture their attention.
