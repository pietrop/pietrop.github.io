in a nutshell, you identify your key online sources, websites, blogs, etc.. collect their RSS Feed into Google Reader and that becomes a searchable database that keeps itself up to date effortlessly.

Now, Google Reader is going to be discontinued some time over the summer, and that poses the problem of what else to use, things like feedly are a good option as a replacement. 

But to go one step forward you could use this RSS Feed research for producing qualitative engagement in your social media outreach, with some sort of editorial control, and build some kind of a "blog". 




Best tool I found so far is scoop.it 
- Ad your RSS Feeds (there's an option to import them from Google Reader or other RSS Feed reader).
- Select keywords searches for youtube, Facebook, twitter, google news.
- Check suggested content, edit (if needed), and re-post in your social media accounts.

and there's a free option that allows up to 5 topics
